The playbook

Six ideas that do the heavy lifting.

Investing well is less about being clever and more about being consistent. Master these and you're ahead of most of the market.

01

Buy the dip.

Fear puts good assets on sale. When the crowd is panic-selling the bottom, that's the discount — not the exit. Be greedy when others are fearful, but buy quality, not falling knives.

02

Time in the market.

It beats timing the market, almost every time. Miss the ten best days of a decade and your returns crater — and those days hide right next to the ugly ones. So you stay in.

03

Automate the boring part.

Dollar-cost average: the same amount, every month, no matter the headlines. It takes the emotion out of the decision and turns volatility from a threat into an ally.

04

Costs compound too.

A 1% fee sounds harmless. Over thirty years it can quietly eat a third of your gains. Low fees are about the only free lunch in investing — take it.

05

Zoom out.

The chart that looks terrifying on the 1-day is a blip on the 10-year. Think in decades, not days — the noise averages out, but the long trend rarely does.

06

Only risk what you can lose.

Especially in crypto. Conviction is great; rent money is not. Size positions so you can sleep at night — and never end up a forced seller at the bottom.
